  1. 1 Seals in force (the offers table) — every arrangement the viewer's crown is party to, plus those publicly known between rivals (the Gallipoli row: broken seals are common knowledge). One row per arrangement; four kinds — alliance, pact, tribute, truce — named in the small-caps kicker of the first column. Parties render as crest pairs (../../art/crests/*.svg in .asset boxes) with the faction name beside each crest — color is never the only channel. Tribute is directional: the gilded arrow reads renders to. Terms are in voice, two lines at most. "Endures" counts down in words ("Until Era III", "Two rounds remain"); rows stand in the order sworn, and the proposed offer sits last, gold-washed, until answered.
  2. 2 Standing pills — In force (laurel), Awaiting your word (gold), Lapsed (plain), Broken (crimson). Each hue is doubled by the word itself. A lapsed or broken row stays in the table until the era turns, so the grudge is legible.
  3. 3 Vassal management — one .vassal-row per crown that kneels: crest, name, since-when pill, and the three obligations (owes / lends / suffers), all enforced by the engine at the phases named — the vassal player does not click to comply. Demand Tribute takes two Gold at once and raises unrest two steps; Ease the Yoke forgives a season's tribute and lowers unrest two steps. Both are ordinary orders (one deed each, ui_click); neither is destructive, so neither confirms.
  4. 4 The unrest gauge — ten steps, tick-marked, filled with the crimson hatch pattern (never a flat hue alone — README §3). The counter and the risk line ("Risk of revolt: rising", with its leading glyph) double the reading. At eight steps or above, revolt may fire in the Council phase and the chronicle writes it (see the vassal-revolt templates in the narrative session's lore). Rising/falling is judged against last round's value.
  5. 5 The scribes' caution — a quiet standing strip, always present on this screen; it is not a toast and never animates. It states the truce-breaking price in voice: two Prestige struck, envoys refused for a round. The Gallipoli row above demonstrates the same figure in the table, so the player has seen the law applied before being asked to swear by it.
  6. 6 The incoming-offer modal — arrives when a rival's offer names the viewer. On the board it is announced by a default toast ("An envoy of the Porte craves audience."); entering the Court opens it over the standard .modal-scrim, exactly as shown. The scrim does not dismiss on click — an envoy is answered, not ignored. The wax seal bears the proposing faction's crest; the offer sentence is quoted verbatim from the proposer, then unpacked in the terms list; the advisor's counsel attends every offer (one bubble, newest counsel replacing old). While the Court is open, crowd_murmur loops (README §4).
  7. 7 Answering — three answers, no more. Seal It (primary) accepts: plays quill_scratch, writes the row into the table as In force, and raises the triumph toast "So it is written." Name Another Price opens the counter-offer composer with these terms pre-filled (composer specced with the trade ledger, a follow-up screen). Send the Envoy Home refuses: not destructive, so no confirmation — the offer lapses and the herald tells the Porte. An unanswered offer expires when the round ends, which counts as refusal.
  8. Spec toggle — the fixed "Dismiss / Recall the Envoy" control at the foot of the viewport is review apparatus (a CSS checkbox), letting reviewers lift the scrim to read the table beneath. It does not ship, and no such dismissal exists in the client.
  9. Sound — crowd_murmur loops for the life of this screen; ui_click on generic presses; quill_scratch on Seal It; page_turn on Return to the Board (README §4).
