  1. 1 The treasury readout — the five resource chips (.resource-chip: icon from ../../art/icons/*.svg, numeral counter, name — icon and word always together, color never the only channel), shared by both leaves and pinned above the tabs. A chip carrying .is-short (Marble here) marks a store some bargain below asks and the vaults cannot meet; it mirrors the barred exchange row. Values refresh the moment any purse is raised or bargain struck, with coin_purse on the change.
  2. 2 The two leaves — "The Free Companies" (the mercenary block) and "The Counting-House" (trade), switched by the CSS-only .tab-group radio pattern from mockups.css §13. No JS in this file. Tab press plays ui_click; the active leaf carries the gold fill per README §1.
  3. 3 The round-timer note — bids close at the Council phase; the counter of heralds' cries remaining ("twice more") ticks each phase. The Council falls at the head of each round's Action phase (design to ratify — the phase track names Omen, Income, Action, Battle). When the hammer falls, each company joins the highest purse; a company no one bid upon stands the block again next round at one Gold less upkeep.
  4. 4 The company cards — always three on the block per round. Each carries the company name, its condottiero, one line of character, and three stats: Strength in dice (die glyphs doubled by words — "four dice afield" — with the host or fleet icon from ../../art/icons/army.svg / fleet.svg), Upkeep in Gold each round, and the Highest Bid with the bidder's crest (../../art/crests/*.svg) beside the worded sum. A company with no bids reads "No purse yet opened." Hover shows the gold rim; the selected card carries .is-selected and aria-pressed.
  5. 5 The bid control — two-step per README §1: the steppers choose the purse (ui_click each press, minus disabled at one Gold above the standing bid, plus disabled at the treasury's edge), and "Raise the Purse" commits it (quill_scratch; triumph toast "So it is written."). Until raised, the Gold stays in the vault and the choice is freely revocable. A purse the treasury cannot bear answers with the error toast "The treasury cannot bear it." Rival bids arriving live update the Highest Bid line and re-floor the stepper.
  6. 6 The monopoly banner — shown whenever any faction holds a trade monopoly touching this house; ground in the holder's faction color via data-faction, doubled by crest and words. Names the holder, the good, and the consequence in one breath. The banner is not a control: monopolies are broken on the board, not in the ledger.
  7. 7 The exchange rows — each row gives one store for another at the house rate, worded in the gold rate pill ("Three Timber for two Gold" — never a bare figure against a figure). Rows touched by a monopoly carry the toll pill in the holder's color. A row the vaults cannot meet is barred: button disabled with the in-voice reason beneath and on hover/tap, row at reduced weight, treasury chip flagged .is-short. Striking a bargain plays coin_purse and refreshes the treasury; rates hold for the round and are re-cried at the Income phase.
  8. 8 The routes held — one lapis pill per route, each naming its ports and worded yield, and a total at the strip's end ("three routes held · four Gold and one Grain each round"). The yield pays itself at the Income phase with coin_purse. A route lost to war or monopoly leaves the strip by way of a herald's toast, never silently.
  9. Advisors — each leaf closes with one .advisor-bubble: Battista Grimaldi (the Bank of Saint George) over the block, Niccolò Spinola (Podestà of Pera) over the ledger. Counsel is flavor, not instruction; it never blocks input.
  10. Sound — ui_click on tabs and steppers; quill_scratch on "Raise the Purse"; coin_purse on bargains struck and route yields; crowd_murmur loops while the market is open (proposed — AUDIO_DESIGN maps that loop to diplomacy; reconcile with the audio team).
