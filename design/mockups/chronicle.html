“The crescent rises over the Golden Horn. Sultan Mehmed takes Constantinople, the two continents are stitched into one dominion, and the Sublime Porte becomes the center of the world.”

The Epilogues

  • The Ottomans.

    From Adrianople (Edirne) to the Bosporus, the road is now all one realm. The City is taken, and Sultan Mehmed is hailed conqueror beneath the great dome.

  • Venice.

    The counting-house falls silent. The routes that fed the lagoon feed a rival now, and Venice watches its ducats sail under another's flag.

  • Hungary.

    The frontier gives way. The marches are overrun, Buda's bells fall silent, and the shield of Christendom is beaten from Hungary's arm.

  • Genoa.

    Genoa is eclipsed. The colonies slip away, the banks fall quiet, and the Superba yields her seat at the world's table to the lion of Saint Mark.

  • Byzantium.

    The Queen of Cities kneels. The cross comes down from the great church, and what was Byzantium passes into memory and lament.

The Chronicle of a Game,
in Three Eras

Set down fair by the scribes, who saw it all and wagered nothing.

Here begins the true chronicle of five powers in a failing age, from the first muster to the fall of the City, fourteen rounds in the playing and fifty-three years in the telling.

Era the First · Rounds I–V · Anno 1400–1420

The Gathering Storm

Renown finds a new favorite. Venice overtakes Genoa and stands first in Prestige. No herald softens it: Genoa takes the field against Venice. Let Negroponte run red.

Venice closes its fist on the trade of Thrace. What flows, flows for it, and the ducats flow after.

Era the Second · Rounds VI–X · Anno 1421–1444

Oaths and Ashes

Thessalonica is taken. The Ottomans' banners climb the walls, and Venice counts the loss in stone and blood.

The seal is broken by King Ladislaus's own hand. Where there stood a pact with Sultan Murad, now there is only the field. The standing shifts in round IX: the Ottomans outpace Venice and stand first in Prestige, 17 to its name.

Era the Third · Rounds XI–XIV · Anno 1445–1453

The Reckoning

In the year 1451, from Adrianople (Edirne), Sultan Mehmed proclaims open war upon Byzantium. The truce is ash; the levies muster. The walls of Selymbria hold no longer. The Ottomans enter as master, and Byzantium is poorer by a city.

Constantinople falls, and with it the age. The Ottomans stand within the walls, and where the City ends, the chronicle can hold no more.

Round XIV · Anno 1453

“On the twenty-ninth of May the crescent rises over the Queen of Cities, and the Roman Empire, that had stood since Augustus, is no more. An age closes with the great doors; the world, not yet knowing it, has become new.”

❦ Here ends the chronicle of this game. Fourteen rounds were played; five powers contended; one City fell. The scribes beg pardon for all errors of the pen. ❦
