  1. 1 Stage and scrim. The campaign board (../../art/map/board.svg) dims under the standard modal scrim; the battle modal centers over it. The scrim does not dismiss on click — a battle, once joined, resolves. Opening plays sword_clash, then battle_distant loops; battle_drums overlays the music bed with campaign_ambient ducked to 30 of 100 (README §4). The vignette in the modal head reuses the event illustration ../../art/illustrations/events/second-kosovo.svg.
  2. 2 The armies arrayed. Attacker left, defender right; each shows crest (../../art/crests/), faction name with data-faction swatch (colorblind hatches per mockups.css §16), levy count as a resource chip (../../art/icons/army.svg), and the commander line. Role is carried by label pill and dice-rim color and position — never color alone.
  3. 3 The dice cascade. One die per levy, less modifiers; attacker row rimmed gold, defender row rimmed lapis; flat parchment faces, ink pips, hard shadows — woodblock, not casino. A die of five or six strikes home. Each die carries a verdict label with a glyph (⚔ hit, · miss): glyph doubling per README §3. Casting plays dice_roll; dice tumble at most 400ms and settle instantly under prefers-reduced-motion. The middle strip is the reckoning: hits weighed against modifiers, then the standing total per side as tally pills.
  4. 4 Modifiers readout. Plain label + side pill + effect sentence in the design vocabulary (dice, hits, the wall bonus). Shown here: the wall bonus at strength two (“Walls II”, shield glyph ⛨) and terrain (“River Crossing”, ≋). Magnitudes are proposed — design to ratify.
  5. 5 The tactic hand. Three cards from the real set (lore/tactics/cards.md): name, one line of flavor, effect. The playable card carries the gold rim and its play button; withheld cards dim to opacity 0.5 and state their reason in voice — a dimmed card must give its reason on hover and tap (README §1). Playing a card plays card_flip. Final card faces render on the frame ../../art/cards/tactic-card.svg.
  6. 6 Result banner. Two states, one shown at runtime in place of the dice cascade: victory (laurel ground, laurel-of-Prestige glyph from ../../art/icons/prestige.svg, horn_fanfare) and defeat (crimson ground, broken-banner glyph ⚑, defeat_drum). Both grounds carry the gold rule and a glyph — color is never the only channel. Closing the banner returns to the board and releases the battle music overlay.
