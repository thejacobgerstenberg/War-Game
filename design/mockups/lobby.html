  1. 1 The word of gathering — the room code, writ large in the display face, generous letter-spacing, always five letters. "Scribe a Copy" places the word on the clipboard (visual mock here — no behavior in this file); on success show the default toast "The word is copied; summon whom you will." and play ui_click.
  2. 2 The seats of the table — always five, in join order. Each seat carries the faction crest (../../art/crests/*.svg in an .asset box), a color band in the faction hue (doubled by the crest — color is never the only channel), the player's name, and a state line. An empty seat reads "A seat awaits" at reduced opacity with dashed borders. A player joining or departing is announced by the herald in the antechamber, never by a toast.
  3. 3 The host's laurel — exactly one seat bears the laurel pill "Host of the Hall". If the host departs, the laurel passes to the eldest remaining seat and the herald announces it. The dashed color band marks a banner chosen but not yet raised.
  4. 4 The banners — five faction cards. All four states are shown statically here: Byzantium at rest; Genoa carrying .is-hover (identical to real :hover — the gold rim per README §1); Hungary carrying .is-selected (gold fill, ink text); Venice and the Ottomans locked — padlock glyph (drawn in CSS, no image), opacity 0.45, cursor: not-allowed, and the note names the claimant. A locked card answers a press only with its note; no toast. In the client, each card is a real <button> inside its <li> — aria-pressed marks the chosen banner, disabled marks a locked one; the role="button"/tabindex here only stand in for that in this static mock.
  5. 5 Raising and beginning — choosing a banner is two-step per README §1: select the card, then commit with "Raise the Banner" (plays quill_scratch; until then the choice is freely revocable). "Begin the Game" belongs to the host alone and stays disabled until three banners are raised; disabled state pairs with the in-voice reason shown beneath and on hover/tap. On press: confirm nothing (beginning is not destructive), play church_bell, and pass every seat to the campaign board.
  6. 6 The antechamber — the lobby chat. Player lines carry the speaker's faction color on the spine and name (doubled by the name itself); herald lines are centered, italic, gold-spined, and machine-authored (joins, departures, banners raised). Input is a mock in this file. Lines cap at two hundred characters; the log keeps the last fifty.
  7. Music — menu_theme loops for the life of this screen (README §4, state LOBBY); it crossfades to campaign_ambient when the game begins.
