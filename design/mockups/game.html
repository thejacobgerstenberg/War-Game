The Contract — zones and behaviors

  1. 1 Round counter & era banner — read-only chrome. Rounds are numbered in Roman numerals; the era banner changes with horn_fanfare over a church_bell tail (README §4). Hover: none. Click: none.
  2. 2 Phase track — five phases: Income, Muster, Campaign, Council, Twilight. Past phases render .is-done (faded), the current phase .is-current (gold fill). Hover a phase for a one-line in-voice tooltip (e.g. "Campaign — hosts march and sieges are set."). Never clickable. The Twilight glyph is commissioned; its path is reserved at art/icons/phase-twilight.svg.
  3. 3 Breath-timer — hourglass and sand-gauge. The gauge drains left-to-right; the caption counts breaths in Roman numerals ("XLII breaths remain"). No clock digits anywhere. Under X breaths the gauge and caption take the crimson of --crimson and the caption pulses once per breath (respect prefers-reduced-motion). At nought the turn yields automatically.
  4. 4 The Treasury (resource rail) — the five stores: Gold, Grain, Timber, Marble, Faith; icon + word + count, always all three (README §3). Hover/tap a chip for the income breakdown by province. .is-short (crimson rim) marks a store that cannot bear the order being lined up, with in-voice hover copy ("The quarries have given all they have.").
  5. 5 Prestige track — nought to twenty; every fifth notch is a gilded milestone. Each faction's marker carries its crest (color is never the only channel). Byzantium stands at eleven. Hover a held notch to read the holder and when it last moved; hover a milestone to read its reward. First to twenty — or the fall of the City — closes the reckoning.
  6. 6 Map viewport — ../../art/map/board.svg, pannable and zoomable (drag / wheel / pinch; keyboard arrows + +/−). Hover a province: gold rim on its path and a name-plate tooltip. Click: select (see 7). Clicking open water clears the selection.
  7. 7 Selected-province ring — gold stroke on the province path itself (drawn here as an overlay mock), persistent until another selection is made. Selection also sets the ring-label name-plate — never color alone. Selecting a province fills the province card (9) and plays ui_click.
  8. 8 Host tokens — one disc per host, in the owner's board color with gold rim, strength in Roman numerals, crest badge at the shoulder. Hover: composition tooltip (levies, siege train, commanding banner). Click: select the host; legal marches shade gold on the map. With March armed (11), clicking a legal province gives the order — committed only at "Set the Seal" (quill_scratch).
  9. 9 Province card — name, owner crest, seasonal yields (all five stores shown, even at nought), works ("Walls II · Harbor"), garrison. Read-only summary; deeper management lives on the province detail screen.
  10. 10 Contextual orders — at most two, chosen by game state (here: Muster a Levy, primary; Strengthen the Walls). Disabled orders keep their place at 0.45 opacity with an in-voice reason on hover ("The levies are spent."). Destructive orders always confirm via .modal.
  11. 11 Action bar — the eight orders: Muster, March, Raise, Traffic, Parley, Whisper, Play a Stratagem, Yield the Floor. One order is armed at a time (.is-selected, gold fill — March here); arming an order sets the map's click-meaning. Yield the Floor is quiet-styled and always confirms. The deed pips show the action budget: four per campaign, spent pips hollow; at nought all orders but Yield the Floor disable.
  12. 12 Event toast — event-card notices dock top-right of the map (ordinary notices keep the top-center .toast-rack, README §2). Thumbnail from ../../art/illustrations/events/, title + one line. Informational toasts yield after about five seconds; clicking opens the full event card. At most one event toast at a time; card_flip on arrival.
  13. 13 Advisor bubble — bottom-left, above the action bar. One counsellor speaks at a time, named and attributed (here Demetrios Choumnos, Grand Logothete of Byzantium). New counsel replaces old; click the bubble to dismiss it. The tutorial speaks through this same seat.
  14. 14 Chronicle drawer — right-edge tab, shown open. Closed: only the gilded "The Chronicle" tab shows. Open: an overlay strip above the map (the shell does not reflow), newest entries first, scrollable within itself, page_turn on open. Entries are the same in-voice lines the end-of-match Chronicle is stitched from.

Callouts and this legend are spec apparatus — they do not ship. The shell above fits 1440×900 with no page scroll; only this legend lies below the fold.
