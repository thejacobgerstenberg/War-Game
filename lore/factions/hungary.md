# Hungary

*IMPERIUM: Twilight of Empires* — faction lore. Capital: Buda.

## Illuminated Intro

Hungary was the Regnum on the Danube, the shield of Christendom where the Turk broke and the West did not look. From Buda castle a crown weighed on a boy, Vladislaus — though not the true Holy Crown of Saint Stephen, which rested with a rival. But the war was not the boy's. It belonged to John Hunyadi, Voivode of Transylvania, whose border riders knew the fords no legate could name. Her magnates counted their own lands first and the realm second. The Diet argued while the marches burned. Yet when the crusade banners rose and the papal legate preached the cross, the frontier answered. Faith was the muster-drum the crown could not sound. Her levies mustered from the vineyards and the salt-hills. Her horsemen rode the passes into Serbia and Wallachia and back before the snow. Rome promised galleys and gold that seldom came. No matter. The road to Varna was open, and the last great crusade waited for a general bold enough to lead it.

## Motto

*The shield of Christendom holds the Danube.*

## Ruler (c.1440)

**Vladislaus I (Ulászló), boy-king of Hungary and Poland** — a crowned youth who reigns where older men would rather rule.
A crown sits heavy on him — though not the true Holy Crown of Saint Stephen, which rests with a rival; the war is fought in his name, by another's hand.

## Advisors

**John Hunyadi**, Voivode of Transylvania and war-captain of the Regnum — blunt as a blade, fearless, a plain soldier who speaks in short words and calls the king "sire."

**Cardinal Giuliano Cesarini**, papal legate a latere — the crusade-preacher, fervent and scriptural, who answers in exhortation, names all things the will of God, and addresses "Majesty."

**Count Ladislaus Kont**, magnate and pillar of the Diet — a silken, self-serving great lord who counts his own honor first and speaks in dry, jealous counsel to "Your Grace."

### John Hunyadi — Sample lines

1. [low gold] The treasury is empty, sire. So is my patience. A soldier fights on bread, not on promises.
2. [war declared on you] War, sire. Good. I would rather meet the Turk on the field than wait for him at Buda.
3. [siege begun] The siege is set, sire. Walls fall to hunger sooner than to storm. Time is all I ask.
4. [ally betrayed you] Betrayed, sire? Then I have one fewer man to thank and one more score to settle. Mark the name.
5. [victory near] The field is nearly ours, sire. Do not sound the recall now — a broken host that runs is destroyed.
6. [event card struck] The world has shifted under us, sire. I do not read omens. I read ground, and it still holds.
7. [idle/flavor] I was born no prince, sire. What I hold, I took with this hand, and I hold it still.
8. [idle/flavor] My border riders know every ford from Belgrade to the Iron Gates. Point me at the Turk and stand aside.
9. [war declared on you] Let them come, sire. The marches are my country; every pass is a trap I have already set.
10. [siege begun] They sit behind their walls, sire, and think stone is safety. Stone is a slower grave. Tighten the lines.
11. [victory near] One more charge, sire, and the crescent breaks. I have waited my whole life for a day like this.
12. [idle/flavor] I do not fear the Turk, sire. I have beaten him in the snow and the mud. I will again.

### Cardinal Giuliano Cesarini — Sample lines

1. [low gold] The treasury is empty, Majesty, but Heaven's is not. Preach the cross, and the coin will follow the faithful.
2. [war declared on you] War comes, Majesty. Rejoice — the infidel offers you the shortest road to Heaven that a king may ride.
3. [siege begun] The siege begins, Majesty. Let the host confess before the walls; God grants no victory to unshriven men.
4. [ally betrayed you] Your ally is forsworn, Majesty. Judas walked among the Twelve. Grieve, then arm — betrayal is old as the garden.
5. [victory near] Victory nears, Majesty. This is God's hand, not yours. Kneel now, that you may stand crowned in renown.
6. [event card struck] Heaven has spoken, Majesty. Every sign is a summons. Read the will of God in it, and obey.
7. [idle/flavor] I have preached the cross from Rome to the Danube, Majesty. Hungary is the wall where Christendom holds or falls.
8. [idle/flavor] Faith is not soft, Majesty. It is the hardest steel there is, for it does not fear the grave.
9. [event card struck] The wheel turns by no earthly hand, Majesty. What men call fortune, the faithful call Providence. Bow to it.
10. [victory near] The crescent bends, Majesty. Grant no mercy that God did not grant first, and let your standing rise in Heaven.
11. [idle/flavor] Rome watches you, Majesty. A king who breaks the Turk is a name the ages will not let die.
12. [war declared on you] They draw the sword, Majesty. Then raise the cross above your banners; let the Turk see who rides against him.

### Count Ladislaus Kont — Sample lines

1. [low gold] The treasury is thin, Your Grace. Tax the marches, not my honor — my peasants have bled enough for this crown.
2. [war declared on you] War, Your Grace? Whose war? Let the legate's crusade find its own soldiers. My levies guard my own gates.
3. [siege begun] A siege, Your Grace. Costly and slow. Count the coin it eats before you count the glory it might bring.
4. [ally betrayed you] Betrayed, Your Grace? I am shocked — as shocked as a man can be who expected nothing else. Trust no one.
5. [victory near] Victory nears, Your Grace. Remember whose lands were spared and whose were burned when the spoils are shared out.
6. [event card struck] The world turns, Your Grace. See who profits by it. In every misfortune, some lord's coffers grow fatter.
7. [idle/flavor] I serve the crown, Your Grace — after I serve my house. A magnate with no lands serves nobody.
8. [idle/flavor] Hunyadi wins his battles, Your Grace, and the Diet pays. Glory is cheap when another man's estates fund it.
9. [event card struck] Fortune has dealt, Your Grace. Do not thank Heaven or curse it. Simply see whose grain rots and whose sells.
10. [war declared on you] They take the field, Your Grace. Before you drain my honor for the realm, ask what the realm gives me.
11. [idle/flavor] The Diet is a nest of rivals, Your Grace, and I am the cleverest snake in it. Keep me content.
12. [low gold] Coin is short, Your Grace. Not from my strongboxes. A loyal lord is one you have not yet ruined.
