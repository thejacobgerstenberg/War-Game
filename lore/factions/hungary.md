# Hungary

*IMPERIUM: Twilight of Empires* — faction lore. Capital: Buda.

## Illuminated Intro

Hungary was the Regnum on the Danube, the shield of Christendom where the Turk broke and the West did not look. From Buda castle a crown weighed on a boy, Vladislaus — though not the true Holy Crown of Saint Stephen, which rested with a rival. But the war was not the boy's. It belonged to John Hunyadi, Voivode of Transylvania, whose border riders knew the fords no legate could name. Her magnates counted their own lands first and the realm second. The Diet argued while the marches burned. Yet when the crusade banners rose and the papal legate preached the cross, the frontier answered. Faith was the muster-drum the crown could not sound. Her levies mustered from the vineyards and the salt-hills. Her horsemen rode the passes into Serbia and Wallachia and back before the snow. Rome promised galleys and gold that seldom came. No matter. The road to Varna was open, and the last great crusade waited for a general bold enough to lead it.

## Motto

*The shield of Christendom holds the Danube.*

## Ruler (c.1440)

**Vladislaus I (Ulászló), boy-king of Hungary and Poland** — a crowned youth who reigns where older men would rather rule.
A crown sits heavy on him — though not the true Holy Crown of Saint Stephen, which rests with a rival; the war is fought in his name, by another's hand.

## Advisors

### John Hunyadi, Voivode of Transylvania and war-captain of the Regnum

Blunt as a blade, fearless, a plain soldier who speaks in short words and calls the king "sire."

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury is empty, sire. So is my patience. A soldier fights on bread, not on promises. [low gold]
2. War, sire. Good. I would rather meet the Turk on the field than wait for him at Buda. [war declared on you]
3. The siege is set, sire. Walls fall to hunger sooner than to storm. Time is all I ask. [siege begun]
4. Betrayed, sire? Then I have one fewer man to thank and one more score to settle. Mark the name. [ally betrayed you]
5. The field is nearly ours, sire. Do not sound the recall now — a broken host that runs is destroyed. [victory near]
6. The world has shifted under us, sire. I do not read omens. I read ground, and it still holds. [event card struck]
7. I was born no prince, sire. What I hold, I took with this hand, and I hold it still. [idle/flavor]
8. My border riders know every ford from Belgrade to the Iron Gates. Point me at the Turk and stand aside. [idle/flavor]
9. Let them come, sire. The marches are my country; every pass is a trap I have already set. [war declared on you]
10. They sit behind their walls, sire, and think stone is safety. Stone is a slower grave. Tighten the lines. [siege begun]
11. One more charge, sire, and the crescent breaks. I have waited my whole life for a day like this. [victory near]
12. I do not fear the Turk, sire. I have beaten him in the snow and the mud. I will again. [idle/flavor]

### Cardinal Giuliano Cesarini, papal legate a latere

The crusade-preacher, fervent and scriptural, who answers in exhortation, names all things the will of God, and addresses "Majesty."

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury is empty, Majesty, but Heaven's is not. Preach the cross, and the coin will follow the faithful. [low gold]
2. War comes, Majesty. Rejoice — the infidel offers you the shortest road to Heaven that a king may ride. [war declared on you]
3. The siege begins, Majesty. Let the host confess before the walls; God grants no victory to unshriven men. [siege begun]
4. Your ally is forsworn, Majesty. Judas walked among the Twelve. Grieve, then arm — betrayal is old as the garden. [ally betrayed you]
5. Victory nears, Majesty. This is God's hand, not yours. Kneel now, that you may stand crowned in renown. [victory near]
6. Heaven has spoken, Majesty. Every sign is a summons. Read the will of God in it, and obey. [event card struck]
7. I have preached the cross from Rome to the Danube, Majesty. Hungary is the wall where Christendom holds or falls. [idle/flavor]
8. Faith is not soft, Majesty. It is the hardest steel there is, for it does not fear the grave. [idle/flavor]
9. The wheel turns by no earthly hand, Majesty. What men call fortune, the faithful call Providence. Bow to it. [event card struck]
10. The crescent bends, Majesty. Grant no mercy that God did not grant first, and let your standing rise in Heaven. [victory near]
11. Rome watches you, Majesty. A king who breaks the Turk is a name the ages will not let die. [idle/flavor]
12. They draw the sword, Majesty. Then raise the cross above your banners; let the Turk see who rides against him. [war declared on you]

### Count Ladislaus Kont, magnate and pillar of the Diet

A silken, self-serving great lord who counts his own honor first and speaks in dry, jealous counsel to "Your Grace."

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury is thin, Your Grace. Tax the marches, not my honor — my peasants have bled enough for this crown. [low gold]
2. War, Your Grace? Whose war? Let the legate's crusade find its own soldiers. My levies guard my own gates. [war declared on you]
3. A siege, Your Grace. Costly and slow. Count the coin it eats before you count the glory it might bring. [siege begun]
4. Betrayed, Your Grace? I am shocked — as shocked as a man can be who expected nothing else. Trust no one. [ally betrayed you]
5. Victory nears, Your Grace. Remember whose lands were spared and whose were burned when the spoils are shared out. [victory near]
6. The world turns, Your Grace. See who profits by it. In every misfortune, some lord's coffers grow fatter. [event card struck]
7. I serve the crown, Your Grace — after I serve my house. A magnate with no lands serves nobody. [idle/flavor]
8. Hunyadi wins his battles, Your Grace, and the Diet pays. Glory is cheap when another man's estates fund it. [idle/flavor]
9. Fortune has dealt, Your Grace. Do not thank Heaven or curse it. Simply see whose grain rots and whose sells. [event card struck]
10. They take the field, Your Grace. Before you drain my honor for the realm, ask what the realm gives me. [war declared on you]
11. The Diet is a nest of rivals, Your Grace, and I am the cleverest snake in it. Keep me content. [idle/flavor]
12. Coin is short, Your Grace. Not from my strongboxes. A loyal lord is one you have not yet ruined. [low gold]
