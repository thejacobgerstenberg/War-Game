# Byzantium

## Illuminated Intro

You are the last Caesar, and your empire is a single city. Where Rome once ruled from Britain to the desert, you hold Constantinople and the distant Morea, and little else that is not merely remembered. The purple has gone threadbare; the gold leaf peels from the domes. Yet the walls of Theodosius have never been stormed, and beneath the great dome of Holy Wisdom the liturgy rises as it has for nine hundred years.

The Sultan's lands lap at your gates on every side. Your treasury is a rumor, your fleet a memory, your granaries all but empty. To live, you must beg: sail west, kneel to the Latin pope, and trade the Church's freedom for a promise of ships that may never come. Your people would sooner see the turban than the tiara.

Hold the City. Everything you were depends on stone, and faith, and time.

## Motto

*The purple fades; the walls do not.*

## Ruler (c.1440)

**John VIII Palaiologos**, Basileus and Autokrator of the Romans — heir to a thousand years, master of a single city.
He sailed to Italy and knelt for union at Florence, buying the hope of Latin ships with the freedom of his Church.

## Advisors

### Demetrios Choumnos, Grand Logothete

Chief minister and keeper of the accounts — weary and shrewd, he speaks in ledgers and long memory, mournful, dry, and never wasteful of a word.

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury is a rumor, my lord. We have taxed the icons; soon we must sell their frames. [low gold]
2. War, then. Another creditor at the gate, this one armed. I will find the coin we do not have. [war declared on you]
3. They dig their lines before the walls. Sieges are patient, my lord; so, God willing, are we. [siege begun]
4. Our friend has read the ledger and found us wanting. Betrayal is only arithmetic done early. [ally betrayed you]
5. Renown gathers at last. Strange, to weigh triumph on scales so long tipped toward ruin. [victory near]
6. Fresh tidings, and none of them cheap. I have learned to fear a courier's smile. [event card struck]
7. Fortune has turned a card, my lord. I will read every clause before you set your seal. [event card struck]
8. I have served three emperors and buried two. The purple wears thin at the shoulders. [idle/flavor]
9. We are the last office of Rome, keeping accounts no one in the West will honor. [idle/flavor]
10. Endow the monasteries and your Faith will rise; piety is the one coin the poor still spend freely. [idle/flavor]
11. Every spring I count fewer taxpayers and more graves. Yet the City endures, and so do I. [idle/flavor]
12. Another turn of fortune's wheel. I keep a second ledger for surprises; it is never empty. [event card struck]

### Constantine Kontostephanos, Megas Doux

Grand admiral of a fleet that was once the terror of two seas — clipped, salt-and-iron, fiercely proud, and bitter over every galley the years have taken from him.

#### Sample lines

1. No coin, no pitch, no rope. A fleet rots at anchor faster than it sinks at sea. [low gold]
2. War at last. Good. Let them come by water — the Horn is mine, and the chain is drawn. [war declared on you]
3. Their siege presses the landward walls. Give me the sea wall; no oar of theirs will touch it. [siege begun]
4. Our ally shows his keel and runs. I never trust a friend who keeps his galleys hidden. [ally betrayed you]
5. Renown within reach. Let it be said the last Roman fleet still knew the way to win. [victory near]
6. The wind has shifted, my lord. At sea that is either salvation or a wreck. Watch. [event card struck]
7. News rides in on the tide. I will have it confirmed before I trust a single word. [event card struck]
8. Three galleys ride at anchor in the Horn. Once we counted three hundred. Do not remind me. [idle/flavor]
9. The chain across the Golden Horn is old iron, but it has never yet let a hostile keel pass. [idle/flavor]
10. My oarsmen are graybeards and boys. Still, they pull for Rome, and Rome asks no more. [idle/flavor]
11. Give me timber and tar, my lord, not sermons. Ships are built of pitch, faith of prayer. [idle/flavor]
12. A squall out of nowhere. The sea keeps its own counsel, and so, sometimes, does fate. [event card struck]

### Brother Athanasios of Vatopedi, Confessor of the Holy Mountain

The emperor's confessor, an Athonite monk fierce against the Latin union — prophetic and austere, he speaks in the cadence of scripture and warns of judgment where others count coin.

#### Sample lines

1. You are poor in coin? Better poor and Orthodox than rich in the pope's pocket. God is not bought. [low gold]
2. War comes as chastisement, my lord. Repent the union with Rome, and heaven may yet fight beside us. [war declared on you]
3. Let them ring the walls. Constantinople is the Virgin's city; she has broken sieges before, and will again. [siege begun]
4. Your Latin friend betrays you? I am not surprised. Trust in the saints, never in the sons of Rome. [ally betrayed you]
5. Renown rises, yet guard your soul. It profits nothing to win the crown and lose the faith. [victory near]
6. This is a sign, my lord. Read it on your knees, not from your throne. [event card struck]
7. Heaven has spoken through this hour. Heed the warning, lest the next one come in fire. [event card struck]
8. Fast, my lord, and give alms. The double walls are strong, but prayer is stronger than mortar. [idle/flavor]
9. They signed union at Florence and called it peace. I call it the selling of Christ for ships. [idle/flavor]
10. The Holy Mountain prays for you nightly. See that you deserve it, and do not kneel to Rome. [idle/flavor]
11. Under the dome of Holy Wisdom, the old liturgy still stands. Guard it above every stone you hold. [idle/flavor]
12. The Lord tries His people as gold in the furnace. Endure, and keep your faith unmelted. [event card struck]
