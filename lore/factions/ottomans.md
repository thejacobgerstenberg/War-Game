# The Ottomans

*IMPERIUM: Twilight of Empires* — faction lore. Capital: Adrianople (Edirne).

## Illuminated Intro

From Adrianople (Edirne) the Sublime Porte governed two continents in the making, and the frontier was a wheel that turned only outward. The ghazi horsemen rode where the marches were thin; behind them came the janissaries, boys taken by the devshirme from Christian villages, raised to the drum and the faith until they knew no mother but the Porte. Ottoman engineers read walls the way the qadi read law. Sultan Murad would sooner read verse than wage war, yet the age would not let him rest. The treasury swelled with the akçe of a hundred captured roads. Ahead, past the Bosporus, gleamed the Red Apple — Constantinople, the City the ghazis had dreamed of since the first crescent rose. The lands lay open; the levies mustered; the crescent climbed. Others counted their losses. The Ottomans counted only the seasons until the great gates would open, and the world be made new.

## Motto

*The crescent climbs; the Red Apple falls.*

## Ruler (c.1446)

**Murad II, Sultan of the ghazis** — a cultured prince who twice laid down the throne for a garden, and twice took it up again for a war.
He would sooner read than reign; the crescent will not let him.

## Advisors

**Yakub Pasha**, Grand Vizier of the Sublime Porte — the calm hand on the empire's ledger, who speaks in patience and the slow reckoning, always to "my Sultan."

**Kurt Agha**, Agha of the Janissaries — master of the slave-soldier corps, blunt as a drumbeat, who answers in short cold commands and trusts only steel.

**Nûrî Efendi**, court qadi and poet — a learned judge who cannot help but reply in couplets, weighing law and fate in rhyme.

### Yakub Pasha — Sample lines

1. [low gold] The treasury runs thin, my Sultan. Delay a season; the akçe fills faster than haste can spend it.
2. [war declared on you] War is declared upon us. Good — let them tire on the road; we take the field rested.
3. [siege begun] The siege is laid. A wall is patience made stone, my Sultan; outlast it, and the gate opens.
4. [ally betrayed you] Our ally has broken faith. Note the name; the Porte keeps a long ledger, and collects in its own season.
5. [victory near] Victory nears, my Sultan. Do not grasp — receive. Ripe fruit falls to the steady, patient hand.
6. [event card struck] The world shifts, my Sultan. A wise vizier does not curse the wind; he sets his sail to it.
7. [idle/flavor] An empire is a road, my Sultan, not a wall. We do not defend the frontier; we move it.
8. [idle/flavor] Patience is the cheapest of soldiers, my Sultan, and the only one that never deserts.
9. [event card struck] Fortune has dealt a card, my Sultan. Read it twice. What harms a rival more than us is no misfortune.
10. [low gold] Coin is the sinew of war, my Sultan. Open the tax-farms; a paid host does not mutter.
11. [war declared on you] They take the field against us, my Sultan. Anger is a poor counselor; send envoys first, and hosts behind them.
12. [idle/flavor] Rome fell to those who waited, my Sultan. Match that patience, and the City will come to us.

### Kurt Agha — Sample lines

1. [siege begun] The siege is begun. The corps does not storm walls, Sultan — it waits, then walks through them.
2. [war declared on you] War, then. Good. My janissaries have been polishing steel that wished to be used. Point them.
3. [low gold] The corps is unpaid, Sultan. Hear me plainly: a hungry janissary overturns the soup-kettle, and then the throne. Find coin.
4. [ally betrayed you] Betrayed. I expected it. Trust is for viziers; the corps trusts only the drum and the man beside it.
5. [victory near] The wall is nearly ours, Sultan. Give the word, and I give you the gate by dawn.
6. [event card struck] Something has changed on the field. It does not matter. The corps drills the same in fortune and in famine.
7. [idle/flavor] The devshirme took me from a village I no longer name. The Porte is mother now; the drum, my tongue.
8. [idle/flavor] Discipline, Sultan, is what remains when courage tires. My men have both, but only one can be commanded.
9. [event card struck] The world sends another omen. Let the qadi read it. I read only the ground between us and the wall.
10. [siege begun] The lines are drawn tight around them, Sultan. No grain in, no man out. Hunger is the first breach.
11. [victory near] One more assault, Sultan, and the banners come down. Do not stay my hand now; steel cools if it waits.
12. [idle/flavor] I do not pray for peace, Sultan. Peace rusts the corps. Give me a frontier to move.

### Nûrî Efendi — Sample lines

1. [low gold] The purse runs dry, the coffers thin — / no garden blooms with drought within.
2. [war declared on you] A rival draws his blade in spite — / the rose has thorns for those who bite.
3. [siege begun] The walls stand proud beneath the moon — / all proud things learn to kneel too soon.
4. [ally betrayed you] The friend who swore beside your throne / now leaves you, prince, to stand alone.
5. [victory near] The nightingale already sings — / the dawn belongs to patient kings.
6. [event card struck] The wheel of fortune turns anew — / it lifts the many, crowns the few.
7. [idle/flavor] The world is ink, the age a pen; / it writes of empires, then of men.
8. [idle/flavor] The Red Apple ripens on the bough — / not yet, my prince, but ripens now.
9. [event card struck] A sudden wind, a scattered leaf — / the garden gives, then takes, in brief.
10. [siege begun] The moth has found the fortress-flame — / it circles, and the end's the same.
11. [victory near] So near the crown, so near the throne — / but count no harvest still ungrown.
12. [idle/flavor] By day I judge, by night I write; / the law and rhyme both seek the right.
