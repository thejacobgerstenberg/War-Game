# The Ottomans

*IMPERIUM: Twilight of Empires* — faction lore. Capital: Adrianople (Edirne).

## Illuminated Intro

From Adrianople (Edirne) the Sublime Porte governed two continents in the making, and the frontier was a wheel that turned only outward. The ghazi horsemen rode where the marches were thin; behind them came the janissaries, boys taken by the devshirme from Christian villages, raised to the drum and the faith until they knew no mother but the Porte. Ottoman engineers read walls the way the qadi read law. Sultan Murad would sooner read verse than wage war, yet the age would not let him rest. The treasury swelled with the akçe of a hundred captured roads. Ahead, past the Bosporus, gleamed the Red Apple — Constantinople, the City the ghazis had dreamed of since the first crescent rose. The lands lay open; the levies mustered; the crescent climbed. Others counted their losses. The Ottomans counted only the seasons until the great gates would open, and the world be made new.

## Motto

*The crescent climbs; the Red Apple falls.*

## Ruler (c.1440)

**Murad II, Sultan of the ghazis** — a cultured prince who dreams of laying down the sword for a garden, yet wields it wherever the frontier calls.
He would sooner read than reign; the crescent will not let him.

## Advisors

### Yakub Pasha, Grand Vizier of the Sublime Porte

The calm hand on the empire's ledger, who speaks in patience and the slow reckoning, always to "my Sultan."

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury runs thin, my Sultan. Delay a season; the akçe fills faster than haste can spend it. [low gold]
2. War is declared upon us. Good — let them tire on the road; we take the field rested. [war declared on you]
3. The siege is laid. A wall is patience made stone, my Sultan; outlast it, and the gate opens. [siege begun]
4. Our ally has broken faith. Note the name; the Porte keeps a long ledger, and collects in its own season. [ally betrayed you]
5. Victory nears, my Sultan. Do not grasp — receive. Ripe fruit falls to the steady, patient hand. [victory near]
6. The world shifts, my Sultan. A wise vizier does not curse the wind; he sets his sail to it. [event card struck]
7. An empire is a road, my Sultan, not a wall. We do not defend the frontier; we move it. [idle/flavor]
8. Patience is the cheapest of soldiers, my Sultan, and the only one that never deserts. [idle/flavor]
9. Fortune has dealt a card, my Sultan. Read it twice. What harms a rival more than us is no misfortune. [event card struck]
10. Coin is the sinew of war, my Sultan. Open the tax-farms; a paid host does not mutter. [low gold]
11. They take the field against us, my Sultan. Anger is a poor counselor; send envoys first, and hosts behind them. [war declared on you]
12. Rome fell to those who waited, my Sultan. Match that patience, and the City will come to us. [idle/flavor]

### Kurt Agha, Agha of the Janissaries

Master of the slave-soldier corps, blunt as a drumbeat, who answers in short cold commands and trusts only steel.

#### Sample lines

1. The siege is begun. The corps does not storm walls, Sultan — it waits, then walks through them. [siege begun]
2. War, then. Good. My janissaries have been polishing steel that wished to be used. Point them. [war declared on you]
3. The corps is unpaid, Sultan. Hear me plainly: a hungry janissary overturns the soup-kettle, and then the throne. Find coin. [low gold]
4. Betrayed. I expected it. Trust is for viziers; the corps trusts only the drum and the man beside it. [ally betrayed you]
5. The wall is nearly ours, Sultan. Give the word, and I give you the gate by dawn. [victory near]
6. Something has changed on the field. It does not matter. The corps drills the same in fortune and in famine. [event card struck]
7. The devshirme took me from a village I no longer name. The Porte is mother now; the drum, my tongue. [idle/flavor]
8. Discipline, Sultan, is what remains when courage tires. My men have both, but only one can be commanded. [idle/flavor]
9. The world sends another omen. Let the qadi read it. I read only the ground between us and the wall. [event card struck]
10. The lines are drawn tight around them, Sultan. No grain in, no man out. Hunger is the first breach. [siege begun]
11. One more assault, Sultan, and the banners come down. Do not stay my hand now; steel cools if it waits. [victory near]
12. I do not pray for peace, Sultan. Peace rusts the corps. Give me a frontier to move. [idle/flavor]

### Nûrî Efendi, court qadi and poet

A learned judge who cannot help but reply in couplets, weighing law and fate in rhyme.

#### Sample lines

1. The purse runs dry, the coffers thin — / no garden blooms with drought within. [low gold]
2. A rival draws his blade in spite — / the rose has thorns for those who bite. [war declared on you]
3. The walls stand proud beneath the moon — / all proud things learn to kneel too soon. [siege begun]
4. The friend who swore beside your throne / now leaves you, prince, to stand alone. [ally betrayed you]
5. The nightingale already sings — / the dawn belongs to patient kings. [victory near]
6. The wheel of fortune turns anew — / it lifts the many, crowns the few. [event card struck]
7. The world is ink, the age a pen; / it writes of empires, then of men. [idle/flavor]
8. The Red Apple ripens on the bough — / not yet, my prince, but ripens now. [idle/flavor]
9. A sudden wind, a scattered leaf — / the garden gives, then takes, in brief. [event card struck]
10. The moth has found the fortress-flame — / it circles, and the end's the same. [siege begun]
11. So near the crown, so near the throne — / but count no harvest still ungrown. [victory near]
12. By day I judge, by night I write; / the law and rhyme both seek the right. [idle/flavor]
