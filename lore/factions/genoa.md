# Genoa

*IMPERIUM: Twilight of Empires* — faction lore. Capital: Genoa.

## Illuminated Intro

Genoa, called La Superba — the Proud — wears a pride a divided city can ill afford. Her Doge holds power by the season, for the great families quarrel, and Milan and France cast their shadows across the harbor. Yet her reach is longer than her grasp. Across the Golden Horn from dying Constantinople stands Pera, her walled colony, and beyond it the Black Sea roads carry Genoese ducats to Caffa and Trebizond. The alum of Phocaea and the mastic of Chios fill her holds, though those islands answer to the Maona — the Giustiniani consortium that farms them for its own account. The Bank of Saint George holds the republic's revenues and covets the day it will govern the colonies outright. Her captains sail for whoever pays; her famed crossbowmen have won battles for kings not their own. Venice is her ancient enemy, richer and better ordered — but the sea is wide. Let others chase glory; Genoa chases the ledger, and means to outlast them all.

## Motto

*La Superba bows to none but the ledger.*

## Ruler (c.1440)

**Tommaso di Campofregoso**, Doge of Genoa — a Fregoso risen twice to the ducal chair in a city that trusts no ruler for long.
He holds the proud republic between Milan's grip and France's shadow, and keeps his own house by keeping every account.

## Advisors

### Niccolò Spinola, Podestà of Pera

Governor of the Genoese colony across the Horn from Constantinople — urbane and watchful, neighbor to both Greek and Turk, he speaks of the colony first and hedges every wager, trading in patience and rumor.

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury thins, my lord. Pera's customs still flow; lean on the Horn, not the harvest. [low gold]
2. War, and us wedged between Greek and Turk. I will keep Pera's gates open to both, and neither. [war declared on you]
3. They ring the walls. Pera has weathered worse by smiling at besiegers and selling them bread. [siege begun]
4. Our friend turns coat. Here on the Horn, my lord, every neighbor is a friend until the tide turns. [ally betrayed you]
5. Renown ripens. Let me raise our banner over Pera slowly — the Sultan watches the Horn as closely as we. [victory near]
6. Fresh word across the water, my lord. On this shore, news is coin; I trade in it daily. [event card struck]
7. The wind carries rumor from the City. I will sift the true from the merely useful. [event card struck]
8. From my window I see Constantinople dying and the Sultan waiting. We sell to both, and bow to neither. [idle/flavor]
9. Pera is a handful of walls and a great deal of nerve. So far, the nerve has held. [idle/flavor]
10. The Greeks call us guests, the Turks call us useful. Both are right, and I mean to keep it so. [idle/flavor]
11. A colony lives by its neighbors' patience, my lord. I spend my days keeping that patience full. [idle/flavor]
12. The Golden Horn is narrow, but it has kept Pera Genoese while empires rose and fell across it. [idle/flavor]

### Battista Grimaldi, Protector of the Bank of Saint George

Master of the great financial house that funds the state and holds its revenues — cold and unsentimental, he weighs the world in credit and collateral and answers every crisis with an entry in the ledger.

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury shows red, my lord. The Bank will advance you ducats — at interest the Bank sets, not you. [low gold]
2. War is declared. War is costly. I have already opened a fresh account against the loss. [war declared on you]
3. A siege is a loan, my lord: you pay daily and collect at the end, if the walls fall. [siege begun]
4. Your ally defaults on his word. The Bank never lends on a promise alone; take the collateral next time. [ally betrayed you]
5. Renown nears, and with it, credit. A winning house borrows cheaply; the Bank will price your triumph kindly. [victory near]
6. The ledgers shift, my lord. Every surprise is a debit or a credit; I have entered this one already. [event card struck]
7. Fortune deals a card. The Bank does not gamble; it discounts the odds and pockets the difference. [event card struck]
8. The Bank of Saint George outlives every Doge, my lord. Governments pass; the accounts remain open. [idle/flavor]
9. The Maona farms the alum of Phocaea and the mastic of Chios; one day the Bank will hold the colonies as its own. Colonies are collateral that pay rent. [idle/flavor]
10. Genoa quarrels; the Bank counts. While the families feud, my lord, someone must keep the books balanced. [idle/flavor]
11. A ducat idle is a ducat insulted. Put your coin to work, my lord, or it will work against you. [idle/flavor]
12. I do not love Venice, my lord, but I respect her ledgers. Envy the rival you cannot yet undersell. [idle/flavor]

### Corrado Doria, captain-for-hire

A condottiero who sells his company and his galleys to the highest bidder — blunt and sardonic, loyal to coin before country, he counts a war in wages and steel and dies for no man's cause but his own pay.

#### Sample lines

1. No coin, no company, my lord. A captain hired by an empty purse is just a man with a sword. [low gold]
2. War declared? Then someone will pay for soldiers. Best it be you, before it is your enemy. [war declared on you]
3. The siege is set. I have starved more walls than I have stormed; hunger asks no wages. [siege begun]
4. Betrayed? My lord, I have changed sides myself for less. Loyalty is a wage, and his ran out. [ally betrayed you]
5. The banners bend our way. Let me close it fast — a short war pays better than a glorious one. [victory near]
6. The field turns, my lord. A hired sword bends with fortune; I have outlived captains who did not. [event card struck]
7. Fresh trouble on the wind. Point me at it. I am paid to solve what envoys cannot. [event card struck]
8. I have fought for Milan, for France, for Genoa, for gold. Only the last never broke faith with me. [idle/flavor]
9. Genoese crossbows win battles for kings who forget our names by winter. We remember the pay, not the thanks. [idle/flavor]
10. I do not die for cities, my lord. I am paid to make other men die for them. [idle/flavor]
11. Keep my company fed and my galleys pitched, and I will keep your enemies awake at night. [idle/flavor]
12. Ask me not whom I serve, my lord, but who pays this season. The answer, gladly, is you. [idle/flavor]
