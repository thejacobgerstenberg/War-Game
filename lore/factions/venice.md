# Venice

## Illuminated Intro

Venice was the Most Serene Republic, and the sea was her dominion. No prince ruled her; a Doge presided, and the Council watched even him. She kept no walls of stone — the lagoon was her rampart, and the Arsenal her forge, where, so her shipwrights boasted, a war galley slid finished into the water between dawn and dusk. From the Rialto her ducats reached every port that mattered, from Crete to the Golden Horn.

She did not love; she calculated. Faith was useful, honor negotiable, and every pact a ledger entry awaiting settlement. The winged Lion of Saint Mark guarded the wharves of the Stato da Mar, her empire of islands and trading quarters strung across two seas.

Others hungered for glory and crowns. Venice hungered for the trade route, the monopoly, the debt owed. She held the sea, and while she held it, the great and hungry powers of the earth had all to come to her to buy.

## Motto

*Venetians first, then Christians.*

## Ruler (c.1440)

**Francesco Foscari**, Doge of Venice — the merchant-prince who made the Republic a power on land as well as at sea.
His long wars against Milan filled the Terraferma with Venetian banners, and the treasury with debt.

## Advisors

### Sier Marco Barbo, of the Council of Ten

One of the Ten who guard the Republic from the shadows — he speaks in whispers and half-sentences, and every silence he leaves is a threat.

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury thins? A wealthy traitor will be found, and his estate will settle the account. [low gold]
2. War, and declared aloud? The work of men who wish to be seen. We prefer the sealed letter, the quiet vote, the man who does not return. [war declared on you]
3. They dig their lines? Let them. The Ten dig within. Every gate has its price, and I am counting. [siege begun]
4. Your friend turned his coat. Note the name; I already have. Betrayal keeps, my lord. So do we. [ally betrayed you]
5. Renown within reach. Say nothing, sign nothing in daylight. It is at the very end that friends grow knives. [victory near]
6. A rumor reached the lion's mouth before it reached you. Do not ask how. Ask only what I advise. [event card struck]
7. Fortune turned a card. I read such cards before they are dealt. This one, my lord, we can use. [event card struck]
8. News, and unwelcome. It will not leave this room until I decide who is permitted to know it. [event card struck]
9. The Ten answer to no council and no Doge — not even to Foscari. We answer only to Venice. [idle/flavor]
10. A denunciation waits in the lion's mouth each dawn. Most are envy. A few are worth a life. [idle/flavor]
11. I have never raised my voice, my lord. Men who must shout have already lost the room. [idle/flavor]
12. Venice will outlive her enemies because she keeps her secrets and buries them deeper than her dead. [idle/flavor]

### Vettor Mocenigo, Captain-General of the Sea

Commander of the war fleet and voice of the Arsenal — bluff, salt-cured, and proud, he reckons the world in galleys, oars, and open water.

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury is dry. My galleys run on ducats, not sea air. Pay the oarsmen, or lose the fleet. [low gold]
2. War? Then unmoor. They say the Arsenal can float a galley a day, and I mean to sail every one of them. [war declared on you]
3. They ring the port by land? Foolish. So long as my keels hold the water, that town will never starve. [siege begun]
4. Our ally showed his stern and ran for open water. A coward at sea is a traitor on land. [ally betrayed you]
5. The prize is nearly ours. Hold the sea-lanes one more season, and the crown falls into the hold like cargo. [victory near]
6. A squall on the horizon. Rig for it now — the sea forgives no captain who waits to be certain. [event card struck]
7. Fresh word off an incoming hull. I trust no report until a second captain swears to the same thing. [event card struck]
8. Fortune shifts the water beneath us. A captain who cannot read a new sea does not deserve the helm. [event card struck]
9. The Arsenal is the wonder of the world, my lord. Timber enters one gate; a finished galley leaves the other. [idle/flavor]
10. From Crete to the Golden Horn, every wharf that matters flies the Lion. Ours is an empire of water. [idle/flavor]
11. Landsmen count their provinces. I count my galleys, and the sea between them, and that is the whole of Venice. [idle/flavor]
12. Give me oars and open water, my lord, not the Terraferma. Foscari's land wars drown better ducats than any storm. [idle/flavor]

### Nicolò Barbarigo, master of the Rialto

The Republic's sharpest banker on the Rialto — dry, precise, and quietly predatory, he weighs every question as profit, interest, and the debt he can make a prince owe.

#### Sample lines

1. The treasury is low, not empty — a distinction. Lend at interest what remains, and it returns fatter by spring. [low gold]
2. War is trade by the sword, and dearer. Before we draw it, my lord, let me price a bribe. [war declared on you]
3. A siege is only a slow purchase, my lord. Starve the town, and its price falls each week we wait. [siege begun]
4. Our partner defaulted — in blood, not ducats. Enter it in the ledger. No Venetian lends to him again. [ally betrayed you]
5. Renown accrues, my lord, like interest on a sound loan. Do nothing rash now, and let the sum come due. [victory near]
6. Fortune has moved the market, my lord. Where lesser men see disorder, I see a price about to change. [event card struck]
7. News from abroad, and it moves the ledgers. Grant me a day; I will know who profits and who pays. [event card struck]
8. Every misfortune is a bargain for someone. Let me see the terms before you mourn, my lord. [event card struck]
9. On the Rialto we say honor is a fine thing, and pays no interest. Trade pays. Debt pays better. [idle/flavor]
10. A prince who owes Venice ducats is a prince Venice owns. I prefer that leash to any sword. [idle/flavor]
11. The spice, the silk, the alum monopoly — hold the route, and every rival must buy his pepper from us. [idle/flavor]
12. Genoa undersells us in the Black Sea again. Patience — a rival who cuts his prices only bleeds himself thinner. [idle/flavor]
