# Loading-Screen & Idle Tips

### *IMPERIUM: Twilight of Empires* (1400–1453)

Forty tips shown on loading screens and during idle turns. Each is tagged by the phase
it serves — `[income]`, `[muster]`, `[campaign]`, `[siege]`, `[diplomacy]`, `[market]`,
or `[any]` — and mixes counsel with true color of the age. Every line follows
`lore/STYLE.md`: chronicle voice, glossary terms, no anachronism, readable in seconds.

---

1. [any] All roads in this age run toward the Bosporus. Whatever you build, build it knowing the City's hour comes.

2. [income] A province at peace pays more than a province under the sword; count the harvest before the glory.

3. [siege] The Theodosian walls turned armies away for a thousand years: a ditch, an outer wall, an inner wall, and a proverb that the City does not fall.

4. [muster] Raise levies before you need them; muster fields are slow, and the field is not.

5. [diplomacy] A truce is a breath, not a peace. Spend it arming.

6. [market] Genoa's Bank of Saint George kept its ledgers so well it ended by governing colonies. Beware a creditor with a fleet.

7. [campaign] One host in the right province outweighs three in the wrong ones.

8. [siege] Orban offered his great bombard to the Basileus first; the treasury was empty, so the Sultan bought it. Mind your treasury.

9. [income] Faith is a harvest like any other; tend it in quiet seasons, for it cannot be bought back in loud ones.

10. [muster] The devshirme took boys from Christian villages and returned them as janissaries; the Sultan's finest levies were born his subjects' sons.

11. [campaign] At Ankara in 1402 Timur broke Sultan Bayezid and took him captive; even empires that seem fated know a dark decade.

12. [diplomacy] The Emperor Manuel traveled as far as Paris and London begging aid for the City; he returned with sympathy, which garrisons nothing.

13. [market] Buy grain when the harvest is fat and sell when the sieges begin; hunger is the market's most faithful customer.

14. [siege] A siege is won in the granary before it is won at the wall; count the defender's grain, then count your patience.

15. [income] The hyperpyra of the last emperors grew thin as leaves; an empire's coin fails before its walls do.

16. [muster] The Arsenal of Venice could send a galley down the ways in a single day, rigged, armed, and victualed. No other yard in the world came close.

17. [campaign] At Varna in 1444 the young king of Hungary charged the janissaries and did not ride back. The crusade died with him.

18. [diplomacy] A vassal pays best when watched and rises fastest when squeezed. Take the tribute; count the knives.

19. [siege] The great bombard needed sixty oxen to haul it and fired but seven times a day. Each shot was a small earthquake, and seven were enough.

20. [market] Venice sailed her merchant galleys in convoys called mude, on schedules fixed by the Senate; even the sea kept Venetian hours.

21. [any] Prestige is won, lost, or squandered — never spent. Gold buys; Prestige persuades.

22. [campaign] János Hunyadi marched the Long Campaign deep into an Ottoman winter and marched home again — which was the harder feat.

23. [income] Genoa drew alum from Phocaea and grain from Caffa; her provinces were counting-houses with walls.

24. [siege] A chain barred the Golden Horn to every fleet — until Mehmed hauled his galleys overland on greased timbers and set them in the harbor anyway.

25. [muster] A host too large to feed is a famine marching under your banner; match the muster to the granary.

26. [diplomacy] At Florence the churches were united on parchment; in Constantinople the crowd muttered it would sooner see the Sultan's turban than the Latin mitre.

27. [market] Marble builds nothing that fights and everything that lasts. Buy it when the wars go quiet.

28. [campaign] Hunyadi lost the field at Kosovo in 1448 and kept the war; great captains outlive their defeats.

29. [siege] In 1452 the Sultan raised a fortress on the Bosporus and named it the Throat-Cutter; the City's grain ships learned the name first.

30. [muster] The Sultan paid his sipahis in land rather than coin, and the timar fed the horseman who guarded it. Levies rooted in a province defend it best.

31. [income] Trade is a deed, not a stockpile; the routes you hold this season are the Gold you count the next.

32. [diplomacy] Genoese Pera stood neutral across the Horn while Genoese Giustiniani bled on the City's walls. Ask which was Genoa's policy.

33. [any] In the siege's last spring the moon rose over the City in eclipse, and the old prophecy stirred. Omens cost nothing and are priced accordingly.

34. [campaign] Never take the field without a road home; retreats are decided before battles are.

35. [siege] In 1453 a small sally-gate, the Kerkoporta, was left unbarred. Great walls fall through small doors.

36. [market] The market keeps no loyalties; if a rival's ducats are good, take them.

37. [muster] Timber laid down in autumn is a fleet by spring; the yard, like the muster field, rewards the early.

38. [diplomacy] Sultan Murad laid down the throne to pray and was called back to fight at Varna; treat no abdication as final, and no truce as retirement.

39. [income] Grain feeds the levies before Gold pays them; a full treasury cannot march on an empty granary.

40. [any] Constantine, last of the Basileis, cast off the purple and fell at the breach. Win or lose, give the chronicle a page worth copying.
