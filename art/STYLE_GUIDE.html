IMPERIUM — Twilight of Empires

Visual Style Guide

Palette

Nine locked colors — no other hex values appear anywhere in the asset library. Tonal variation is achieved with opacity only. Two auxiliary neutrals are reserved for crest charges and highlights.

Typography

Two faces carry the whole game: Cinzel for display — Roman square capitals cut for inscriptions — and EB Garamond for body text and map labels. Referenced by name only; never embedded.

In the year of our Lord 1453 the double-headed eagle kept watch over a city of ghosts. Galleys of the Serenissima slipped past Genoese watchtowers at Galata, grain barges crept along the Bosphorus, and in the hills of Rumelia the sultan’s engineers dragged bombards toward the Theodosian walls. Prestige is the only currency history honors — gold, grain, timber and marble merely purchase it.

Icons

Thirteen single-color glyphs — four resources, five actions and assets, four round phases. Every mark is currentColor, so a single CSS color swap restyles the full set.

Unit Tokens

Seven board pieces — four land units, two galleys, and the army-stack badge. Like the icons they are pure currentColor (shown here in ink); the badge's central disc carries a live count slot, #army-stack-badge-count-slot, that the client rewrites at runtime (“3” is the authored placeholder).

Buildings

Eight 48×48 structures: five improvements — market, temple, university, shipyard, harbor — and the three wall tiers, timber palisade to Theodosian double wall. All currentColor, drawn to read at map scale.

fortification escalation — wall I → II → III; each tier keeps the previous silhouette’s footprint and adds mass

Faction Crests

Five playable powers, each a 200×240 shield beneath its wreath and scroll. Auxiliary white and silver appear only here, as heraldic charges.

the same crests at 48 px — token scale; each silhouette must stay legible

Faction Patterns

Colorblind-safe ownership overlays: every faction is distinguishable by geometry alone — Byzantine lattice, Ottoman crescents, Venetian waves, Genoese checker, Hungarian chevrons — so the map stays readable under any color-vision deficiency and in grayscale print. The demo sheet below is art/patterns/faction-patterns.svg verbatim: transparent 12–15 px tiles (pattern-byzantium … pattern-hungary) that composite over the faction tint, plus a usage blob showing tint coat + pattern overlay + label.

Card Frames

Three 300×420 frame templates share one anatomy — Greek-key border, title ribbon, art window, body panel, footer emblem — and differ by accent color and emblem: crimson omen-eye for Events, lapis crossed swords for Tactics, gold-and-laurel wreath for Objectives.

UI Chrome

The digital client’s furniture: a 9-slice parchment panel, one button in its four states, tooltip and modal frames, the phase banner, and the six-face battle die. Every frame is parchment-on-ink with gold fittings; nothing introduces a new color.

The Board

The Mediterranean, 1400–1453, drawn as a portolan chart: rhumb lines, compass rose, meander frame, and sea monsters in open water. 55 land provinces and 12 named sea zones, matching docs/MAP.md exactly; strait markers at the Bosphorus, Dardanelles, Messina, and the Golden Horn; the Danube drawn course-true past Buda and Belgrade.

Byzantium Ottomans Venice Genoa Hungary Minor / Independent

The board ships with the historical 1453 opening position pre-applied: every <path class="province"> in art/map/board.svg already carries its start-owner class — owner-byzantium, owner-ottomans, owner-venice, owner-genoa, owner-hungary, or owner-minor — exactly as registered in docs/MAP.md §4. Recoloring in play is one class swap; fills flow from the --faction-* custom properties at 55% fill-opacity, so the parchment grain reads through.
